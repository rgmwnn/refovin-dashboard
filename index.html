Section 06

Analytical Conclusion & Underwriting Recommendations

Synthesized analytical findings and actionable underwriting guidance derived from the cohort risk analysis and anomaly deep dive.

RevoFin's portfolio is broadly healthy at the headline level, with a very high TKB30 of 99.9929% and very low Default OS Rate of 0.0071%. However, risk is concentrated in specific cohorts and borrower/loan segments.

The key analytical distinction is: 2013 = rate-based anomaly because it has the lowest TKB30 at 99.8485%. 2016 = exposure-based anomaly because it contributes 74.43% of total Default OS.

The 2016 deep dive suggests that risk is not driven by one single factor. Instead, it appears to be associated with selected states, loan purposes such as credit card and debt consolidation, higher interest rate segments especially >20%, and shorter-to-mid employment tenure groups. Because only 12 loans are defaulted, these findings should be interpreted as directional risk signals, not causal conclusions.
01
Monitor TKB30 and Default OS Separately
Do not rely only on TKB30. A cohort can have a strong TKB30 but still contribute material Default OS. TKB30 identifies rate-based quality deterioration, while Default OS identifies material financial exposure. Both lenses are required for complete portfolio risk assessment.
02
Prioritize 2016 for Underwriting Review
The 2016 cohort contributes 145.35K Default OS, or 74.43% of total Default OS. Review loan approval patterns, borrower characteristics, and pricing decisions in this cohort. This is the highest-priority analytical action.
03
Strengthen Purpose-Based Risk Segmentation
Credit card loans in 2016 are disproportionately represented in Default OS (22.58% OS share but 44.29% Default OS share). Apply tighter affordability checks or differentiated approval thresholds for purposes that show disproportionate default contribution.
04
Review High-Interest-Rate Borrower Segments
The >20% interest rate bucket accounts for only 9.40% of 2016 OS but contributes 39.87% of 2016 Default OS. Pricing alone may not fully offset borrower risk. Consider additional underwriting overlays for high-rate approvals.
05
Add State-Level Portfolio Monitoring
2016 Default OS is concentrated in states such as KS, MI, TX, AZ, CA, and NY. KS in particular contributes a high share of Default OS relative to portfolio size. Introduce state-level portfolio monitoring and regional underwriting review protocols.
06
Treat Profile Findings Cautiously
Default loans are only 12 records, so segmentation-level rates can be volatile. Use these findings as early risk indicators and validate with larger historical samples. Avoid applying policy changes based solely on these signals without broader validation.
